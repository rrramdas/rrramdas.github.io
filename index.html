Ludwig says: 1. The world is all that is the case. 2. What is the case—a fact—is the existence of states of affairs. 3. A logical picture of facts is a thought. 4. A thought is a proposition with sense. 5. A proposition is a truth-function of elementary propositions. (An elementary proposition is a truth function of itself.) 6. The general form of a truth-function is [p, ξ, N(ξ)]. This is the general form of a proposition. 7. What we cannot speak about we must pass over in silence.
